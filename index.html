Call for PapersGenAI4Health @ NeurIPS 2026

About

Following the successful first GenAI4Health workshop at NeurIPS 2025 (https://genai4health.github.io/), the third GenAI4Health workshop convenes machine learning researchers, healthcare professionals, policy experts, and clinical practitioners to advance capable, safe, equitable, and trusted GenAI for health.

Sydney, Australia. December 6-12, 2026.

Topics of Interest

We invite submissions of original, unpublished work related to Generative AI in healthcare.

1
Frontier Models for HealthAdvances in agentic medical AI, clinical reasoning, multimodal learning across text, imaging, audio, video, waveform, and EHR data, retrieval-augmented generation, diagnosis, prognosis, triage, care planning, tool use, clinical LLMs, clinician copilots, and longitudinal patient modeling.
2
Trustworthy AI, Policy, Human-AI Collaboration & AdoptionSafety, robustness, fairness, responsible deployment, regulatory compliance, human oversight, clinician-in-the-loop systems, red teaming, explainability, bias mitigation, surgical AI, FDA and international regulatory alignment, and adoption in clinical workflows.
3
Toward 360° AI Care: Ambient, Conversational & Embodied Systems for HealthVoice agents, ambient scribes, conversational AI, longitudinal patient interaction, AI companions and coaches, virtual care navigators, multimodal interactive systems, context-aware assistants, care coordination, and embodied or agentic healthcare interfaces.

Camera-Ready Submission Guidelines

  • Review Feedback: Please carefully address all reviewer feedback and thoroughly proofread your paper for clarity and consistency.
  • LaTeX Format: Format your paper using the NeurIPS 2026 LaTeX package with the following workshop options: \usepackage[dblblindworkshop,final]{neurips_2026}.
  • Workshop Title: Include the workshop title in your paper by adding: \workshoptitle{The Third Workshop on GenAI for Health: Agentic Systems, Clinical Trust, and Future Potential}.
  • Public Availability: Your camera-ready paper will be made publicly available on OpenReview after TBA, unless you contact the organizers before the deadline to opt out.

Submission Tracks

Research, demonstration, and position papers may address any of the three topic areas. We encourage multidisciplinary submissions involving stakeholders from healthcare, public policy, and adjacent fields.

Research Papers

The core of the program, presenting methodological advances and empirical evaluations. Fewer than 9 pages, excluding acknowledgments, references, and appendix.

Demonstration Papers

Working systems or applications. Fewer than 5 pages, excluding acknowledgments, references, and appendix.

Position Papers

Perspectives or proposals on policy, governance, or deployment strategies. Fewer than 5 pages, excluding acknowledgments, references, and appendix.

Submission Format Requirements

  • You must format your submission using the NeurIPS 2026 LaTeX style file. The NeurIPS Paper Checklist is not required.
  • Use \usepackage{neurips_2026} without options to ensure the submission is anonymous.
  • All page limits exclude acknowledgments, references, and appendix.
  • Papers may be rejected without consideration of their merits if they fail to meet the submission requirements.
  • We encourage multidisciplinary submissions involving stakeholders from healthcare, public policy, or adjacent fields to ensure practical relevance and responsible innovation.
  • Supplementary materials (code, data, videos) may be submitted as appendices.
  • Papers submitted to the workshop must not have been previously published at another venue at the time of submission.
  • The accepted papers will be non-archival (NOT included in proceedings or any form of publication).
  • Non-archival status means papers can be submitted to other venues after the workshop.
  • Accepted workshop papers (after the camera-ready stage) will be made publicly available by default. However, authors may choose to opt out, in which case their paper (PDF file) will not appear on OpenReview. A reminder will be sent to authors before the camera-ready stage.

Review Process

  • Submit through OpenReview; the submission portal will be announced.
  • Each paper will receive three reviews.
  • Accepted papers will be non-archival.

Presentation & Awards

  • One Outstanding Paper Award will be selected for each paper type.
